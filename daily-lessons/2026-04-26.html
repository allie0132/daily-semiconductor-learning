TSV Continuity Testing in HBM Stacks

Saturday, Apr 25 2026

Why TSV Continuity Matters

Through‑Silicon Vias (TSVs) are the electrical backbone of HBM stacks. Any open, shorts, or resistance drift can cause link training failure, degraded bandwidth, or heat hotspots. JEDEC JESD235‑B defines continuity as RTSV ≤ 5 mΩ for nominal 25 µm diameter Cu vias at 25 °C.

Test Structures and Probe Setup

Use the dedicated TSV_CONT register block (base address 0x8000_0000) provided in the HBM controller’s BIST ROM. The interposer probe card must present +5 V bias on the TSV reference pad and GND on the opposite side.

  • Probe tip impedance: 50 Ω, < 5 ps skew.
  • Contact force: 30 g ± 5 g per TSV, verified by on‑board load cells.
  • Temperature control: 25 °C ± 0.5 °C during DC measurement; up to 85 °C for stress tests.

DC I/V Continuity Measurement Procedure

1. Program TSV_CONT_CTRL (0x8000_0010) with MODE=DC, VSET=0.1 V.
2. Enable TSV_CONT_START (0x8000_0014).
3. Read back TSV_CONT_RESULT (0x8000_0018): bits[15:0] = measured resistance (µΩ).

Pass criteria: RES ≤ 5000 (i.e., ≤5 mΩ). If fail, repeat with VSET=0.5 V to detect high‑impedance opens.

Time‑Domain Reflectometry (TDR) for Open Detection

When DC resistance is marginal, launch a 2 ns step edge (±10 ps jitter) via TSV_TDR_CTRL (0x8000_0020). Capture the reflected waveform on the ATE’s high‑bandwidth sampler (≥20 GHz, 10 ps resolution).

  • Open signature: ~1.5 ns delay spike, amplitude > 0.8 V.
  • Short signature: immediate (<200 ps) high‑amplitude (<1.2 V) reflection.

Compare to JEDEC‑defined reference plots (JESD235‑B Figure 8‑12).

Temperature‑Stressed Continuity Sweep

Cycle the stack from –40 °C to 125 °C in 25 °C steps while repeating the DC measurement.

Record ΔR per step; a slope > 0.2 mΩ/°C indicates possible Cu electromigration or interposer delamination. Log data to TSV_TEMP_LOG (0x8000_0100‑0x8000_011F) for trend analysis.

⚡ Key Takeaways

  • JEDEC JESD235‑B caps TSV resistance at 5 mΩ; use TSV_CONT registers for direct measurement.
  • TDR complements DC I/V by revealing high‑impedance opens or shorts with sub‑nanosecond resolution.
  • Temperature sweeps expose latent defects; a resistance slope >0.2 mΩ/°C is a fail indicator.
